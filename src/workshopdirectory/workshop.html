Lesson Plan Title: How to recognize red flags in every relationship

Goals and Objectives

  1. Participants will learn what a red flag is in a relationship and how to discern red flags.
  2. Participants will be able to learn how to date responsibly.
  3. Help participants identify unhealthy relationship behavior early, understand why they matter and know what healthy alternatives look like.

Opening Prayer and introduction(5minutes)

I appreciate you all for the effort you made to be present in this workshop. We will begin by inviting a Muslim woman and a Christian woman to bless us with an opening prayer. Afterwards we introduce ourselves so that we get to know and address each other by names.

Objectives of class(5minutes)

What is a red flag in a relationship context, Identify 6-8 common behavioral red flags across romantic, friendship and family relationships. Be able to distinguish between a red flag, yellow flag and a normal conflict. Describe 2-3 steps to respond if they notice red flags in their own relationships. The proposed use items for teaching include white board, markers, handout (Family Proclamation to the world). Anonymized scenarios for group activities.

Asking of questions(20minutes)

Think of a friendship or relationship that felt off but you could not explain why at the time. What was 1 thing that felt wrong? Collect 3-4 anonymous answers on the board. I won't judge them yet. This sets the context that gut feelings matter.

Lesson Flow(20minutes)

Marriage is a vocation to holiness. From the first time you meet as husband and wife through the time together married couple have those awesome tasks of witnessing to Gods faithful love to each other, their children, and society. No couple does this perfectly, and everyone needs help when love feels strained and the going gets rough and tough. All marriages can grow in knowledge, faith, joy and love. Whether you just started dating, recently married or you have spent years together or you are divorced or even a single mother this workshop is for you. What is a red flag? A red flag is a behavior or pattern that suggests potential harm, disrespect, or an unhealthy power dynamic. It's not proof that someone is bad, but it's a signal to pay attention and set boundaries. Red flags are behavior that violate respect, safety, or consent. This needs to be addressed or it's a reason to step back. Yellow Flag: Annoying or concerning but might be fixable with good communication skills. Green flags: The are behavior in a relationship that builds trust, respect and safety. Core content: Break red flags into categories. For each with an example and 1 healthy alternative. control and isolation in relationship: Him or her telling you who you can see, checking your phone, making you feel guilty for having other relationships. Green flag: Encourage your independence and friendship. Disrespect of Boundaries in relationship: Ignoring No; pressuring you after you say stop, showing up uninvited. Green flags: Accept No without guilt-tripping. As part of the revelation received in the last day by a prophet is the family proclamation to the world that state that individuals who violate covenant of chastity, who abuse spouse or offsprings, or who fails to fulfill family responsibilities will one day stand accountable before God.(Prophet Gordon Hinckley.,1995).

Class Activities(5minutes)

  1. Group the class into various groups (3).
  2. Allow them to watch a short video.
  3. Ask them to explain and discuss what they observed from the short video.

Class Assessment(3minutes)

  1. Write down 1 red flag you now recognize, and 1 green flag you want in your relationships.
  2. Briefly write down what you have learnt in this class.
